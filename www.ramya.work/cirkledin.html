CIRKLEDIN

Platform to build and maintain student portfolio

Cirkledin - A platform to track students' activities and achievements

Link: Cirkledin.com     Stakeholder: Reetu Gupta (Founder)

Cirkledin is an online platform to track students’ activities and achievements all in one place. It is a Linkedin for high-school students. It helps students showcase their strengths, talents and accomplishments for college admission, scholarships, internships, summer job and other opportunities of their choice.  I was brought in to redesign their core product and website. I led efforts to evolve the product and address customer pain-points to improve the overall experience.

 

MY ROLE:

UX Designer

 

TASKS:

Wireframing, Hi-fi Prototypes,

Identity design, Website design

Marketing materials

 

 

The Vision – A friendly student portfolio

The goal was to design a product where a student will feel motivated and secure in documenting his/her activities on an on-going basis. Competitors out there either had a complicated product or were focusing on different goals than student success. I decided to redesign the product to keep it simple and at the same time provide clear paths and visual cues to delve deeper as needed. Though we agreed that the website had to be optimized for mobile, I focused on designing for the web (mainly from desktop and laptop computers) for the duration of this project.

 

The Approach - Understanding teen behavior

Based on user research, users (students) expected professional sites to look simple and polished. We decided on presenting content in a clear & interesting manner rather than experimenting with new and sophisticated features. Our user research findings resonated with the design guidelines for engaging teens by Nielsen Norman Group.

 

Working with a team – Good, fast and cheap

In favor of bringing the product to market quickly, we decided to leverage the existing architecture of the product. This early decision helped move quicker. I led the design effort and delivered mockups in one week sprints. Design and development were broken into parallel work streams. Since we were working on a tighter schedule, my work involved designing for the next feature in the pipeline, while simultaneously working with the engineering team to execute current feature development to completion.

 

Thinking on Paper

The design stage started with paper prototypes. My process involved sketching, wireframing and white boarding the interface. Sketching helped obtain a better view of the system and ensure a cohesive design. After discussing the flow with the stakeholder, I moved onto hi-fidelity designs on Sketch. I also used Axure and made videos to communicate interactions in the design. For each feature, I went through discussing requirements, sketching, approval, detailed specs and delivery. 

 

Breaking it down - A simpler flow

Existing usage patterns indicated that

1. Users don’t complete the entire profile all at one go. They complete the basic information for all the categories and then delve deeper into specifics

2. Users are more inclined to complete the “academics” section first and then move on to other categories – say sports, extra curricular, summer activities etc.

3. Adding media and other supporting documents followed

Based on these insights, I designed the editing experience to bring up only the necessary fields to create a section. This decision decreased the cognitive load for the user and uplifted their emotion by showing an increased profile completion percentage each time they completed an activity.

 

Maintaining brand identity

Cirkledin already had a strong visual identify. I leveraged the existing visual guidelines to build the product further. In addition, we also decided to bring in some bright energetic colors to interest the younger user segment. 

 

Let users control what they share

Teens are cautious about what they share on the web. We put them in charge of what/how they share their profile. We gave users the ability to “hide” parts of the profile. The interaction was designed in such a way that users see their profile closest to how it appears in “public” view at all times.

Results

After the redesign, the new website met with great reviews from the users. This design served as the base for extending the experience to mobile screens. The previous website was a good proof of concept, but the one I designed will provide a solid foundation to extend the product further.
