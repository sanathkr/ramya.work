KARDS

An Online Wedding Invitation Company

Kards - Say N0 to boring wedding invitations

Link: Kards.in 

I started Kards, an online wedding invitation company focusing on the Indian market. Indian brides are moving away from printed stationary to digital e-cards for their wedding. We wanted to capitalize on this trend and provide an online service to customize and download a wedding invitation.  My goal was to design a product that was easy to use and motivate the users to complete the purchase online without seeking a designer's help.

The Challenge: First to market

Indian brides were not used to the idea of buying wedding invitations online. Our goal was to create a product that was fun, easy and useful for my customers. Based on extensive user research, I came up with a persona to constantly guide me through and create empathy during initial stages. I conducted several rounds of user research to gain insights into the needs of the customer.

 

Design for user’s emotional state

Wedding is an emotional affair. Users wanted to feel “special” in the process of customizing and downloading a card.  I mapped out the user’s state of mind to understand the emotional state I had to design for. I was thrilled by the opportunity to create something that was first of its kind in the market.

 

Approach – Mobile First

Based on user research, I identified that majority of our users started the customization experience from their mobile and completed it on desktop website. This understanding helped me focus on designing the interactions right on both mobile and website from the beginning. Paper sketches helped visualize ideas about the UI, functional and data elements in the product.

 

Early user testing

Sketch was the choice of prototyping tool because I wanted a quick feedback from the user on the direction we were heading. I developed a high fidelity prototype with majority of the interactions defined. I tested this prototype with 10 users. During user testing, I realized that users loved the experience of customizing the card. However, I also picked up on the “lack of confidence” in designing the card without professional help.

Increase user confidence - The winner

After a lot of brainstorming and design exercises, editor was redesigned to have a clean & simple interface. This, combined with few other tweaks resulted in an experience that instilled confidence in the user. We decided on showing just the right mix of color/font/design options, enough to excite, but not overwhelm the user. We also encouraged the user to share a digital proof with their friends and family before completing the purchase. This change made a huge positive difference in the way user's interacted with the website.

Establish brand's visual identity

Color palette and design decisions were chosen to establish Kards as a unique, quirky and modern brand, giving a breath of fresh air for the otherwise boring wedding card industry. We made sure to carry the same visual tone to marketing materials and social media content as well. The brand identity grew to be strong to an extent that users started referring to the e-cards with the brand name "Kards".

 

Results: Over 500 paying customers in a month

The website was a great hit and received a lot of positive reviews from the users. Kards saw over 500 paying customers in the first month of launch. Kards was also featured in leading wedding planning websites as the go-to website to get quirky wedding e-cards.
