SMARTSHEET

A Collaborative Work Management Tool

Smartsheet - A platform to track, automate, and report on work

Link: Smartsheet.com     

Stakeholder: Kit Unger, Senior Director of UX, Smartsheet

Smartsheet is a Software as a Service (SaaS) company offering collaborative work management solutions. They are losing users after the 30-day trial period ends.

This was a short-term consulting project. My team and I were asked to redesign the Onboarding process to increase the conversion rates from trial to paid subscriptions. Over a 3-week period, we successfully designed a user-guided Onboarding process, which increased the overall learnability and usability of the product. We presented our findings to the CEO of the company.

 

MY ROLE:

UX Designer - Consultant

(Duration: 3 weeks)

 

TASKS:

User Research, Usability study

On-boarding flow, Visual design

Clickable Prototype
 
 

The Opportunity

Convert free users to paid customers

Smartsheet offers a 30-day trial period for first time users. The company receives a significant amount of sign ups for the free trial. However, after the trial period ends, very few users actually convert to “paid” customers. We learned from our stakeholder interview that Smartsheet has identified several user pain paints around the product. Our usability tests also confirmed the pain points. Based on this background, our goal was to address the usability problems while keeping in mind the bigger goal of increasing the overall emotional experience of the users when interacting with the product.

 

THE APPROACH

Started with a usability study on the existing user flow

We conducted usability tests with five participants for 30 minute sessions each. After the study, we made an affinity diagram to capture how users felt while using the product and also developed our persona. Following are the top three research highlights:

1. Tool tips overwhelmed the users and was not very helpful in guiding them through the product

2. Users were confused between different icons in the tool bar

3. Users failed to discover key features of Smartsheet

 

A customer journey map led to the key objectives for the redesign

We decided to Introduce tool tips in more timely and unobtrusive way and resign the tool bar to increase discovery of key features.  The goal was to build personality into the product to motivate the user.

 

User-guided tutorial to introduce key features

We held a design studio to address each of the redesign objectives above. Our team generated a lot of sketches which became the foundation for building our high fidelity prototype. We built an interactive user-guided tutorial that helps the user to get started with the product right away. We also redesigned the tool bar to distinguish icons that looked similar.

 

Visual thinking on paper eventually led to the Medium fidelity prototypes

We built our Medium fidelity prototype in Axure and conducted 10 usability tests to validate our findings. Through the tests, we saw that our new design solution increased discoverability and learnability of key features. Users developed confidence in and commitment to the product. Based on our usability tests, we drafted a new customer journey map depicting the increased positive emotional experience of the users. 

The Impact

The redesigned website and onboarding flow was met with outstanding reviews by company members, partners, and customers, while bringing in more customers. The redesigned icons resonated well with the customers and made navigation easier for first time users of the product.
